What to consider before submitting a copyright complaint<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

Before submitting a copyright complaint to us, consider whether the use could be considered fair use. Fair use states that brief excerpts of copyrighted material may, under certain circumstances, be quoted verbatim for purposes such as criticism, news reporting, teaching, and research, without the need for permission from or payment to the copyright holder. If you have considered fair use, and you still wish to continue with a copyright complaint, you may want to first reach out to the user in question to see if you can resolve the matter directly with the user.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Please note that if you are unsure whether the material you are reporting is in fact infringing, you may wish to contact an attorney before filing a notification with us.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

We may, at our discretion or as required by law, share a copy of your notification or counter-notification with third parties. If you are concerned about your information being forwarded, you may wish to use an agent<\/a> to report infringing material for you.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

You may refer to a\u00a0DMCA takedown notice generator<\/a>\u00a0or other similar services to avoid making mistakes and ensure compliance of your Notification.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Filing a DMCA complaint is the start of a pre-defined legal process. Your complaint will be reviewed for accuracy, validity, and completeness. If your complaint has satisfied these requirements, our response may include the removal or restriction of access to allegedly infringing material as well as a permanent termination of repeat infringers\u2019 accounts.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
